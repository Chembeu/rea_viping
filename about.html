Our History and Background

REA Vipingo Plantations Limited was incorporated in Kenya in 1995 and comprises the company, which has four wholly owned subsidiaries:

  • Dwa Estate Limited – owns the Dwa estate
  • Amboni Plantations Limited – owns the three Tanzanian estates
  • Amboni Spinning Mill Limited – owns the Spinning Mill in Tanzania
  • Wigglesworth Exporters Limited – a warehousing and shipping operation based in Mombasa, Kenya

The company’s origins date back to the early 20th century, with a rich heritage in sisal cultivation and processing. Over the decades, REA Vipingo has expanded its operations across Kenya and Tanzania, becoming a key player in the global sisal industry.

Our commitment to quality, sustainability, and innovation has enabled us to maintain leadership in the market while supporting local communities and promoting environmental stewardship.

With an annual sisal fibre production of over 17,000 tonnes, the company is by far the largest sisal producer in Africa. Other than some fibre that is used in the group’s sisal spinning mill in Tanzania, all fibre produced is exported.

Our Sisal Estates

Dwa Estate

The Dwa Estate, located at Kibwezi some 200 kms southeast of Nairobi, is the largest estate in the group - with a land area of 8,990 hectares and an annual fibre production of over 7,000 tonnes. Dwa fibre is sold under the well respected Chai mark.

Vipingo Estate

Vipingo Estate located about 30kms north of Kenya’s second city, Mombasa. The annual sisal fibre production of Vipingo is around 3,500 tonnes.

Tanzania Estates

The group owns three sisal estates in northeastern Tanzania—Mwera, Sakura, and Kigombe—located 30 to 70 kilometers south of Tanga. These estates produce approximately 7,000 tonnes of sisal fiber annually. Most of the fiber is exported via the port of Tanga, except for a portion used in the group's Amboni sisal spinning mill.

Amboni Spinning Mill

REA Vipingo Plantations owns and operates the Amboni Spinning Mill located on the outskirts of Tanga in north eastern Tanzania. The mill is equipped to produce a full range of sisal yarns, twines and ropes to internationally accepted quality standards.

The Amboni Spinning Mill produces sisal yarns in the range of 150 metres per kilo to 900 metres per kilo and sisal ropes in the diameter range of 5 mm to 40 mm. Yarn and ropes are available on a variety of different package sizes.

Yarns produced by the mill are used in carpets, buffing and polishing cloth, steel wire cores, sisal ropes, agricultural twine, general packing and binder twines, as well as other woven product. Sisal rope produced by the mill is used for agriculture, mining, nautical and other uses.
